Bosco Rangmanch - Drama

Writeup

        "All the world's a stage, and all the men and women merely players."
        Welcome to Bosco Rangmanch, where the streets transform into stages and every
        corner becomes a canvas for storytelling. So, join us as we embark on a journey
        through the ages, where each performance will serve as a window into the past, a
        reflection of the present, or a glimpse into the infinite possibilities of the future.

Rules

        Participants: 5+2

        1.	The use of vernacular is permitted when required. Any profane or inappropriate language will lead to immediate disqualification.
        2.	No microphones, collar microphones or plug-in facilities will be provided by the host school.
        3.	The participating schools need to bring their own physically aided instruments, make-up and props like banners and placards. 
        4.	No props will be provided by the host school.
        5.	The host school bears no responsibility for any instruments or props brought by the school, in case it is misplaced or destroyed.
        6.	The decision of the judges and the organisers will be considered final and binding in all cases. Indirect political references are allowed.
        7.	However, no specific mentions must be made to any political entity, dead or alive. No vulgarity will be accepted in regards to 
        attire. The event has been divided into two rounds: 
        
        Round 1: 
        
        1.	This round would entail enacting hypothetical, alternate endings for popular movies.
        2.	The participating schools will receive the movie titles for the play one week before the fest. 
        3.	Time limit: 8+2 minutes. 
        4.	Number of Participants: 5+2 actors. 
        5.	The soft copy of the script needs to be mailed to the Fest e-mail id (home.boscofest@gmail.com).
        6.	A hard copy of the same has to be submitted at the registration kiosk on the day of the fest. 
        7.	The participants are advised to refrain from referencing the original ending of the movie, and making drastic
         changes to the main plotline of the movie (before the last act). Top five schools from Round 1 will qualify for Round 2 
        
        
        Round 2:
        
        1.	The Round would assess the participants spontaneous wit and storytelling prowess in a series of themed improvisations, 
        creating captivating scenes on the spot. 
        2.	The participants will draw chits for the setting/topic and characters in the scene, and will perform with the chosen prompts.
        3.	Five schools who were in the leading in the previous round will be qualified to participate.
        4.	Time limit: 2+1 minutes.
        5.	Number of Participants: 3 actors.
        6.	No pre-written scripts, or use of external sources like the internet, will be allowed. Participants are encouraged to 
        have a coherent plot/narrative within the scene. Repetition of dialogues/actions could cause negative marking.
